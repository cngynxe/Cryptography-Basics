The Chi-Squared Statistic

The Chi-Squared Statistic is a generic measure of how similar two categorical probability distributions are. The larger, the chi-squared statistic is, the more the the distributions of the two probabilities vary.

Note that the variables C & E stand for the counts of the letters themselves, not the probabilities.

The Chi-Squared statistic is most often used to find the "shift" in substitution ciphers (specifically the Caesar cipher). By comparing the the encrypted text's letter distribution to the normal English letter distribution, we can identify the shift key of the cipher.
